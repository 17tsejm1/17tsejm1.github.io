Mathematics

 
 
 
 

Learning Aims

 

This course will develop students’ ability to recognize and appreciate patterns and relationships, and to investigate and explore different areas of mathematics. Students will be expected to create, read and understand mathematics and then write and discuss what students have discovered with other students. In solving problems students will also be expected to present their solutions clearly, showing full working, and to check and interpret results.

Students will develop their ability to reason logically, to classify, to generalize and to prove. As students gain knowledge they will learn how to apply their skills to solving practical problems in a variety of contexts. The course will also show students how mathematics can be used in their other subjects, particularly science and technology, as a tool for solving many different types of problems.

The course is also intended to provide students with the appropriate foundation so that they will have the necessary mathematical skills for future studies and employment.

Course Content

The IGCSE course is divided into twelve modules covering a range of topics including:

Number
Algebra
Geometry Trigonometry Statistics & Probability

The IGCSE course will be challenging but enjoyable and rewarding as well.

Assessment

IGCSE International Mathematics (CIE 0607)

At the end of the IGCSE mathematics course students will sit three examination papers, at either the Core or Extended level. Paper 1(2) will be a non-calculator paper, Paper 3 (4) will require the use of a graphing calculator, and Paper 5(6) will involve solving one or two long problems by using an investigative approach and/or by using mathematical modelling. Graphical calculators are also required for Paper 5(6).

Level Grade Papers Available

There are two levels of the IGCSE Mathematics Course, with a “Core” and “Extended” level.

The Core Examination will last a total of 3.5 Hours, and students will be able to receive a C grade at best. It consists of paper 1, 3 and 5.

The Extended Examination will last a total of 4.5 Hours, and students will be able to receive a A* Grade at best. It consists of paper 2, 4 and 6.

IGCSE Additional Mathematics (CIE 0606)

A proportion of students will be entered into an Additional Mathematics course, as well as the IGCSE above. This course will further develop their mathematical concepts and principles. It will extend their mathematical skills and will enable their use in more advanced techniques. Students will have more ability to solve problems, present solutions logically and interpret results. This will give students a more solid foundation for Higher Level IB Study. As this course is more demanding and mathematically challenging, it will be on offer to approximately the top 25 percentile of students. The course will run parallel and during normal Mathematics classes.

Examination Structure

The examination is split into two papers, with both papers having 10 to 12 questions, and with no choice of questions. The total amount of time available for completing the two papers is 4 hours, and the grades available are 80 per paper, adding up to a maximum of 160 for both papers.
