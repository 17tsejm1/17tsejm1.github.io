An estimated amount of 200,000 people die in the province of Wallo as a result of famine.

A famine returns to Wollo, in the northern provinces. The failure to adequately handle this crisis led to the fall of the government and led to the Derg Rule.

1973-1974

Haile Selassie deposed in coup led by Teferi Benti.

Haile Selassie was overthrown by a coup led by Teferi Benti. After this Benti was appointed as the head of state of Ethiopia and the chairman of the Derg.

1974

Haile Selassie dies while in custody.

Emperor Haile Selassie allegedly died of respiratory failure under mysterious circumstances while being held in custody by a coup led by Teferi Benti.

1975

Benti killed and replaced by Mengistu Haile Mariam.

Terefi Benti is murdered and replaced by Mengitsu Haile Mariam. On the 3rd of February 1977, gunfire erupted during a meeting of the standard committee of the Derg.

1977

Thousands of government opponents die in a “Red Terror”

Thousands of government opponents die in Red Terror orchestrated by Mengistu while collectivisation of agriculture begins. The Tigrayan People's Liberation Front launches war for regional autonomy.

1977-1979

Somalia invades Ethiopia's Ogaden region.

The Ogaden War was a conflict that was created between Ethiopia and Somalia during the period of July 1977 to March 1978 over the disputed Ogaden region in present-day eastern Ethiopia.

1977

Somali forces defeated with massive help from the Soviet Union.

The Soviet Union switched from aiding Somalia against Ethiopia to supporting Ethiopia in the Ogaden War, which had previously been backed by the United States. This in turn prompted the US to later start supporting Somalia. The war ended when Somali forces retreated back across the border and a truce was declared.

1978

Mengistu Haile Mariam sentenced to death.

Mengistu Haile Mariam was found guilty of genocide in absentia in January 2007 and sentenced to life in prison for the deaths of between 30,000 to 500,000 civilians. Unfortunately, the sentence has not been realized as Mengistu remains in exile in Zimbabwe. In 2008 the Ethiopian Supreme Court sentenced him in absentia to death and handed down guilty verdicts to approximately 30 of Mengistu’s associates and accomplices to heavy prison terms or death. This is a list of 60 senior government officials, civil servants, decorated war veterans and elite army officers summarily executed by the Derg firing squad Mengistu Haile Mariam was in charge of on 24th November, 1974.
