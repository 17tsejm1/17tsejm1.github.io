The Ethiopian Red Terror is a genocide worth remembering because of the staggering amount of people that had been killed by the Derg in the span of one year. Creating memorials also allows us to remember all of the victims of this genocide. It all started in 1974, when the Derg brought an end to the imperial rule. Not long after, the execution of many ancien regime members marked the start of the bloodshed. The Red Terror has claimed over 265,000 lives. Although this may not be a significant number, remember that this amount of people died in the span of one year as The Red Terror only lasted for a period of one year from 1977 to 1978. The Red Terror had brought an end to imperial rule in 1974 were accompanied by slogans of a ‘bloodless Ethiopian revolution’, which by the beginning of the urban terror, had already become a distant and uncertain memory.

Our memorial is a website about this Red Terror. We have chosen the colours red and black as the theme for our website because black symbolises death and red symbolises the blood spilled in this genocide. We think that this website is an effective memorial because this website will be public so anyone with access to the internet can see the work that we have done and learn something from it. We thought that this would be the most effective way of doing it as over 40% of the world’s population have access to the internet, making it easily accessible to a large percentage of the public.
