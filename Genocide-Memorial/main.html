This Genocide & This Memorial

Why?

This should answer most, if not all of your questions.

Why did we choose this genocide?

We chose this genocide because we believed that people needed to be educated about this genocide, after all, it is one of the genocides within the top five in amount of deaths. We found that there were many first person accounts from this genocide, but the information that was avaialable was scattered everywhere and it caused us some trouble, but the harder we searched, the more information we found about this genocide. And what we found horrified us, it was a capatalist country until the Communist Mengistu Haile Haram took control of the Derg, the military on the 3rd of February, 1977. This then caused a chain of incidents that caused anywhere between 30,000 to 500,000 deaths, and this number is still unclear today.

Why is this genocide worth remembering?

The Ethiopian Red Terror is a genocide worth remembering because of the staggering amount of people that had been killed by the Derg in the span of one year. Creating memorials also allows us to remember all of the victims of this genocide. It all started in 1974, when the Derg brought an end to the imperial rule. Not long after, the execution of many ancien regime members marked the start of the bloodshed. The Red Terror has claimed over 265,000 lives. Although this may not be a significant number, remember that this amount of people died in the span of one year as The Red Terror only lasted for a period of one year from 1977 to 1978. The Red Terror had brought an end to imperial rule in 1974 were accompanied by slogans of a ‘bloodless Ethiopian revolution’, which by the beginning of the urban terror, had already become a distant and uncertain memory.

Why did we choose to create a memorial in this form?

Our memorial is a website about The Red Terror Genocide in Ethiopia. We have chosen the colours red and black as the theme for our website as black symbolises death while red symbolises the blood spilled in this genocide. We think that this website is an effective memorial because this website will be public so anyone with access to the internet can see the work that we have done and learn something from it. We thought that this would be the most effective way of doing it as over 40% of the world’s population have access to the internet, making it easily accessible to a large percentage of the public, allowing us to educate more people quickly, should they want to learn about it.
